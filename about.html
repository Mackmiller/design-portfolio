Mackenzie is professionally motivated by her passions for accessibility and effective communication. Her international professional experiences across multiple industries contribute to her continued interest in cross-discipline collaboration and technology integration/accessibility.


The Engineer

Mackenzie's previous work across multiple industries contributes to an analytical yet creative skillset for learning and applying new technology, specifically for improving the usability and accessibilty of web-based user experiences. In her free time, she enjoys testing out various data visualization libraries, learning Web3, and going down the occasional blackhole about algorithmic musical composition. Mackenzie is currently employed as a software engineer within the financial services industry.


The Designer

Mackenzie holds two degrees from the University of Miami, most recently a Master of Fine Arts with emphasis in data visualization and user experience design. Her research and projects during this degree revolved around applying user-centered design to the development of data-driven web applications and physical computing topics. After graduation, Mackenzie consulted as a visual communications designer primarily for US-based nonprofits, and her work has been utilized in the continental US, Europe, and Africa. In addition to her role in these projects as lead designer/coder, Mackenzie enjoyed gaining valuable project manager experience.


The Classical Musician

Prior to her masters, Mackenzie graduated from the Frost School of Music at the University of Miami, where she received her Bachelor of Music in Instrumental Performance. While living in South Florida for eight years, Mackenzie was an active flute soloist and pedagogue, maintaining a virtual and in-person flute studio consisting of students ages 8 to 85. In addition to her formal training at UM, Mackenzie worked with international flute soloist Sir James Galway from 2017-2019 in Switzerland, where she was a flute soloist and artistic administrator at his summer institute.
